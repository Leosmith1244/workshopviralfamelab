Our Expert Speakers & Guides

Meet the Toastmasters ready to help you crack the code of viral content.

Speech 1: "The 3-Second Rule"

Speaker: Club VP of Public Relations

Objective: Explain why social media content gets skipped in 3 seconds and how to hook audiences using Aristotle’s Rhetorical Triangle.

Outline:

  • Introduction (1 min): Hook with relatable frustration, establish credibility, and preview the problem and solution.
  • Body (4-5 min):
    • The 3-Second Problem: Data on viewer skipping (TikTok analytics).
    • Why Content Flops: Common mistakes (boring visuals, wordy intros).
    • Three Fixes: Bold visuals, punchy intros, clear value.
  • Conclusion (1 min): Recap, call to action to create contagious content.

Activity 1: Live TikTok Critique (4 min): Analyze pre-selected TikTok posts, identifying what makes them hook or flop, linking to Ethos, Pathos, and Logos.

Speech 2: "Dopamine Hacks"

Speaker: Club President

Objective: Teach how to trigger shares by evoking outrage, awe, or FOMO using Aristotle’s Rhetorical Triangle.

Outline:

  • Introduction (1 min): Hook with a viral scenario, share a success story, preview the strategy.
  • Body (4-5 min):
    • Why Emotions Drive Shares: Psychology of dopamine and sharing (Harvard Business Review).
    • Three Dopamine Hacks: Outrage, Awe, FOMO (examples).
    • Real-World Success: Club example of boosted attendance.
  • Conclusion (1 min): Recap hacks, inspire vision of flooding social media.

Activity 2: Rewrite a Dull Post (4 min): Rewrite a bland club post to include emotional triggers in breakout rooms.

Speech 3: "The Dark Arts of Clickbait"

Speaker: Club Secretary

Objective: Share ethical clickbait techniques to create irresistible headlines for Toastmasters posts.

Outline:

  • Introduction (1 min): Provocative question, personal win, preview formulas.
  • Body (4-5 min):
    • Why Headlines Matter: Data on clickbait engagement (BuzzSumo).
    • Three Clickbait Formulas: Listicles, Questions, Bold Claims (examples).
    • Ethics and Impact: Stress on delivering on promises.
  • Conclusion (1 min): Recap formulas, rally to craft contagious headlines.

Activity 3: Create a Scandalous-But-True TM Headline (4 min): Write ethical, attention-grabbing headlines for club events, with voting for the most contagious.

Speech 4: "Trend-Jacking Olympics"

Speaker: Club VP of Education

Objective: Show how to leverage trending memes or topics to promote Toastmasters.

Outline:

  • Introduction (1 min): Humorous scenario, club success story, preview steps.
  • Body (4-5 min):
    • Why Trends Work: Data on engagement (Sprout Social), amplification.
    • Three Trend-Jacking Steps: Monitor trends, adapt to club context, post quickly.
    • Real Impact: Club example of boosted guest attendance.
  • Conclusion (1 min): Recap steps, rally to hijack trends for club promotion.

Activity 4: Meme Rebrand Race (4 min): Teams rebrand a trending meme for club promotion, sharing ideas in chat.
